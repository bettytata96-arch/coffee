About Us

Coffee in Ethiopia isn’t just a drink, it’s a whole personality. The beans grow wild in the highlands, soaking up sun and mist like they’re posing for a magazine cover. Every region has its own flavor story. Yirgacheffe is bright and floral, Sidamo leans smooth and sweet, and Harrar hits you with blueberry notes like it’s showing off. People roast and brew it as part of a traditional ceremony that can take an hour, but nobody complains because the whole thing feels like a slow ritual of patience and warmth. When you sip Ethiopian coffee, you’re tasting where coffee was born, shaped by mountains, soil, and people who treat it with real respect.
